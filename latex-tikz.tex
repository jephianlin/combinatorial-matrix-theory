Consider the equation $x^2 + x + 1 = 0$.  One may use the quadratic formula to find the zeros  
\[\frac{-1+\sqrt{3}}{2} \text{ and } \frac{-1-\sqrt{3}}{2}.\]

Let 
\[A = \begin{bmatrix}
 1 & 2 & 3 \\
 4 & 5 & 6 \\
 7 & 8 & 9
\end{bmatrix}.\]
Then $A\bv = \bzero$ is equivalent to the system of linear equations
\[\begin{aligned}
 x + 2y + 3z &= 0, \\
 4x + 5y + 6z &= 0, \\
 7x + 8y + 9z &= 0.
\end{aligned}\]
